# Phase 1 Report: Contrastive LSTM on Toy Logs

Branch: `contrastive-phase1`
Status: **Discontinued**. Code archived at [archive/legacy/contrastive_phase1/](../../../archive/legacy/contrastive_phase1/).

---

## Objective

Explore whether a simple self-supervised contrastive encoder could learn useful embeddings from
synthetic log files, providing a foundation for operating-mode detection without labels.

---

## What Was Tried

### Architecture

- **Encoder**: LSTM (`src/version1/models/contrastive.py` â†’ archived)
  - Input: window of preprocessed log feature vectors
  - Hidden dim: 128 (configurable via `configs/contrastive_toy.yaml`)
  - Projection head: 2-layer MLP â†’ 64-dim unit sphere
- **Loss**: NT-Xent (normalised temperature-scaled cross-entropy) with temperature 0.1
  (`src/log_to_vec/training/contrastive_losses.py`)
- **Positive pairs**: temporal neighbours (consecutive or near-consecutive windows within the
  same trajectory). This was the central methodological choice and the central flaw.

### Data

- `data/toy_logs.csv`: CSV with columns `timestamp`, `event_type`, `component`, `severity`,
  `message`, `data` (JSON). Generated by `examples/toy_log_generator.py`.
- `examples/generate_sine_logs.py`: sine-wave synthetic signals encoded as log-like CSV.
- Preprocessing: categorical encoding + normalisation via
  `src/log_to_vec/data/preprocessor.py`. Output saved to `data/preprocessor.json`.
- No factorised latent structure; no known ground truth.

### Training Setup

- Config: `configs/contrastive_toy.yaml`
  - batch_size: 64, hidden_dim: 128, projection_dim: 64, temperature: 0.1
- End-to-end script: `examples/complete_workflow.py`
- Training: `examples/train_contrastive_toy.py`, `examples/train_contrastive.py`

### Evaluation

- Ad-hoc only: `archive/legacy/contrastive_phase1/src/version1/evaluation/`
  - `evaluate_embeddings.py`: global statistics, L2 norm, variance
  - `clustering_test.py`: k-means on embeddings, no labelled ground truth
  - `retrieval_test.py`: nearest-neighbour retrieval, no factor labels
  - `visualize_embeddings.py`: t-SNE and PCA plots
- No linear probes, no retrieval precision by factor, no clustering ARI.
- Outputs: `outputs/version1/evaluation/embedding_evaluation_summary.json`

## Why It Was Discontinued

1. **No ground truth**: without known latent factors there is no way to measure whether the
   embedding captures anything useful. Visualisations alone are not evidence.

2. **Temporal-neighbour pairs cannot produce factor-aware structure**: the model learns that
   consecutive windows are similarâ€”which is trivially trueâ€”but this does not require learning
   mode identity, spectral family, coupling, or load.

3. **Toy data is too simple**: the sine-wave and toy CSV generators do not have factorised
   regime structure. There is no meaningful mode to detect.

4. **No comparison to baselines**: without baselines, even a partially useful embedding cannot
   be distinguished from trivial temporal smoothing.

---

## Key Lessons

- **Lesson 1**: Temporal-neighbour positive pairs are fundamentally misaligned with factor
  recovery. Do not use them as the primary positive pair strategy unless the goal is temporal
  smoothness, not factor recovery.

- **Lesson 2**: A controlled synthetic benchmark with known latent structure (factorised modes,
  spectral families, coupling, load) is necessary before any claim about learned representations.

- **Lesson 3**: Evaluation must include labelled probes, retrieval precision by factor, and
  clustering ARI against known labels. Visualisations and norm statistics are insufficient.

- **Lesson 4**: The original log data (text + categorical + JSON) has structure that pure LSTM
  contrastive learning over numerical features does not capture. The log-to-numerical-feature
  conversion step loses event semantics.

---

## What to Reuse

- The log preprocessor (`src/log_to_vec/data/preprocessor.py`) is the correct approach for
  converting log CSV to numerical features. It is production-ready and tested.
- The NT-Xent loss implementation (`src/log_to_vec/training/contrastive_losses.py`) is correct
  and reusable.
- The probe/retrieval/clustering evaluation framework developed in Phase 2 and 3 should be used
  to retrospectively evaluate any Phase 1 style embeddings if re-run with proper data.

---

## What NOT to Repeat

- Running contrastive training on toy logs without labelled factors and calling it an evaluation.
- Using temporal-neighbour pairs as the only positive-pair strategy.
- Evaluating with visualisations only and claiming structure is learned.
